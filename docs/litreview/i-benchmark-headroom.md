## Literature report: benchmark sensitivity/saturation for optimizer comparisons

### (1) Closest works (citation → contribution → implication for testbed choice)

1. **Wen, Hall, Ma, Liang — "Fantastic Pretraining Optimizers and Where to Find Them", [arXiv:2509.02046](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.02046)** (Stanford/Marin, 2025). Ten optimizers, 0.1B–1.2B params, 1–8x Chinchilla, per-optimizer HP tuning. Findings: claimed 1.4–2x speedups shrink to **~1.4x at 0.1B and ~1.1x at 1.2B** for matrix optimizers (Muon/SOAP) over well-tuned AdamW; rankings **flip during LR decay**, so only end-of-training comparisons at the target budget are valid; blind HP transfer between optimizers is unfair. Notably, they do **not** report seed counts/error bars — their resolution claims rest on effect sizes being large (≥1.1x). *Implication: a 130M-param, ~1x-Chinchilla LM run is the smallest published setting where optimizer-family deltas are demonstrably large (1.3–1.4x), i.e., real headroom.*

2. **Semenov et al. (EPFL/Jaggi) — "Benchmarking Optimizers for Large Language Model Pretraining", [arXiv:2509.01440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01440), code: [epfml/llm-optimizer-benchmark](https://github.com/epfml/llm-optimizer-benchmark)**. 124M/210M/583M/720M dense + 520M MoE, ~2,900 runs, 24+ optimizers incl. D-Muon. Protocol recommendations: decay LR to 1% of max (under-decayed baselines inflate challenger gains), large decoupled weight decay matters, warmup and scheduler choice are optimizer-dependent, re-tune β₂ for longer horizons; rankings change with batch size and horizon. Seeds: only wall-clock timings get 5-seed error bars; loss comparisons largely single-run. *Implication: their 124M configs are a ready-made, reproducible small testbed; but their own results show ranking instability across batch/horizon — pin one regime.*

3. **Kaddour, Key, Nawrot, Minervini, Kusner — "No Train No Gain", NeurIPS 2023, [arXiv:2307.06440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.06440)**. Under a compute-equivalent, fully-decayed-LR baseline, gains from efficient-training methods (incl. Lion, Sophia) **vanish** on BERT/T5 pretraining. Introduced "reference system time" protocol. *This is the closest published precedent for "claimed optimizer gains disappear under a properly matched baseline."*

4. **Dahl et al. — "Benchmarking Neural Network Training Algorithms" (AlgoPerf) + Kasimbeg et al. — "Accelerating neural network training: An analysis of the AlgoPerf competition", ICLR 2025, [arXiv:2502.15015](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15015)**; [results](https://mlcommons.org/benchmarks/algorithms/). Design rationale: **time-to-target** on fixed hardware (not loss-at-fixed-steps), aggregate across workloads, held-out workload variants to punish benchmark overfitting/co-adaptation. Resolved deltas: Distributed Shampoo **28%** faster than tuned NAdamW baseline; gap to 2nd place only **~2%** — so the benchmark resolves few-percent time-to-target deltas, using ~10 seeds per comparison plotted individually (Schedule-Free evaluation practice). *Implication: time-to-target metrics restore sensitivity that fixed-step accuracy metrics lose; but full AlgoPerf is thousands of dollars — only single cheap workloads fit $25.*

5. **Jordan — "94% on CIFAR-10 in 3.29 Seconds on a Single GPU", [arXiv:2404.00498](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.00498)**. The airbench design paper itself is explicitly a **statistical-power argument**: typical inter-run std **0.14pp**; detecting a 0.02pp mean improvement at p=0.05 needs **N≈133 runs**, which airbench makes cheap (7.3 GPU-min). Airbench94_muon: 94.01% mean, 2.59s. *Implication: airbench was designed to resolve tiny deltas via massive n — but it says nothing about whether optimizer swaps produce any delta at the record config; your MDE 0.04pp at n=100 is exactly consistent with his 0.14pp std.*

6. **"Muon with Nesterov Momentum: Heavy-Tailed Noise and (Randomized) Inexact Polar Decomposition", [arXiv:2605.06884](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06884)** (2026). On a compact CIFAR-10 CNN, Muon variants reach "essentially the same test accuracy," nearly indistinguishable from each other, and the authors attribute the narrow separation to **limited model capacity restricting gains from orthogonalization** over well-tuned baselines. *The closest published statement to your saturation finding — but qualitative, without your n=100/MDE quantification and not on the co-adapted record config.*

7. **Bouthillier et al. — "Accounting for Variance in ML Benchmarks", MLSys 2021, [arXiv:2103.03098](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03098)**. Randomize *all* variance sources (data order, init, HP search) rather than many seeds of one source; gives a 51x-cheaper unbiased comparison estimator and shows single-source seed variance understates true comparison uncertainty. *Implication for your protocol: your 100-seed equivalence is strong for init/data-order variance but silent on HP-co-adaptation variance — which is exactly the axis the record config is overfit on.*

8. **Mishchenko (Meta) — modded-nanogpt baseline tightening, 3,325 → 3,250 steps ([coverage](https://digg.com/tech/4onr95p4))** (2026): "weak baselines can make any idea look promising" — issued after an undocumented DistributedShampoo stability flag inflated Shampoo results on the speedrun. *Published critique that speedrun-adjacent optimizer claims are baseline-tuning artifacts.*

9. **Prime Intellect — [autonomous nanoGPT speedrunning](https://www.primeintellect.ai/auto-nanogpt), "Track 3" (optimizer-only)** (2026): fixed architecture/data, only optimizer+schedule+init+WD may change; step-count-to-loss-3.28 metric; human Muon baseline **2,990 steps**, best agent records **2,930–2,950** (~1.3–2%), with an explicit **statistical noise floor to prevent seed hacking**. ~10k runs / 14k H200-hours spent to find ~2%. *Two implications: (a) the optimizer-only margin on a co-adapted speedrun config is only ~2% — i.e., the config is nearly (not fully) saturated for optimizer-family substitutions; (b) the track demonstrates that 1–2% step-efficiency deltas ARE statistically resolvable there with modest per-comparison run counts.*

10. **Modded-nanogpt record-validation practice ([repo](https://github.com/KellerJordan/modded-nanogpt), [snimu record writeups](https://snimu.github.io/2025/10/07/modded-nanogpt-value-embeddings.html))**: submissions must show **p<0.01** that mean val loss ≤ target over enough runs; a medium-track record published all **19 run losses** (mean ≈2.9194, σ ≈ **0.0006**). *Published seed-variance numbers you can power-calculate against.* Related follow-ups worth knowing: "Fantastic Pretraining Optimizers II: Hyperball" ([arXiv:2606.16899](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.16899)), "Towards Robust Scaling Laws for Optimizers" ([arXiv:2602.07712](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07712)) — per-optimizer scaling-law fits are brittle under resampling; "Hyperparameter Transfer Enables Consistent Gains of Matrix-Preconditioned Optimizers Across Scales" ([arXiv:2512.05620](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.05620)) — muP-style transfer as the route to trusting small proxies.

### (2) Verdict on novelty

**Your specific result is not published; the surrounding claim is partially published.** Published pieces: (a) qualitative "Muon variants indistinguishable on small CIFAR CNNs, capacity-limited" (arXiv:2605.06884); (b) "efficient-optimizer gains vanish vs compute-matched decayed baselines" (No Train No Gain); (c) "claimed speedups shrink under tuned baselines and with scale" (2509.02046); (d) weak-baseline/co-adaptation critiques of the speedrun ecosystem (Mishchenko; AlgoPerf's held-out-variant design exists precisely because configs co-adapt); (e) general benchmark-saturation/ceiling-effect analyses ([arXiv:2602.16763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16763)) — but those target model-capability benchmarks, not optimizer testbeds. **No one has published a powered equivalence result on a record/speedrun config** ("all Muon-family variants within 0.03pp, MDE 0.04pp, n=100"). That is novel quantified evidence and is publishable as a methods finding — especially framed with Bouthillier-style variance accounting and the Track-3 observation that the co-adapted nanogpt config leaves only ~2% optimizer-only headroom. Caveat to pre-empt in review: on airbench the metric is accuracy-at-fixed-steps against a near-capacity ceiling; equivalence there does not show the optimizers are equivalent, only that this metric/config can't separate them (the 2605.06884 capacity argument).

### (3) Ranked cheapest testbeds with headroom (~$25, A100/A6000-class spot)

1. **Modded-nanogpt 124M, optimizer-only ("Track 3") protocol, steps-to-val-loss-3.28 on FineWeb.** Justification from published numbers: optimizer-only records separate by 1–2% steps above a statistical noise floor; record practice publishes run distributions (σ ≈ 0.0006–0.002 loss at target; p<0.01 acceptance criterion), and Muon's own headline claim (35% fewer steps than tuned AdamW) was established here — so optimizer-family effects of 1–3% steps are both present and resolvable with n≈5–15 per arm. Cost: current-gen short-track runs are ~2–4 min on 8xH100 (~$1–1.5/run on ~$16–20/hr spot; ~$25 buys ~2 arms x 8 runs). If only 1xA100 is available, the same config with grad accumulation runs ~1.5–2.5h (~$3–4/run), still ~6–8 runs. Measure **steps-to-target** (or loss-at-fixed-steps converted via the local loss/steps slope), not wall-clock.
2. **124M–130M Llama/GPT-2 at ~1x Chinchilla on FineWeb with the epfml [llm-optimizer-benchmark](https://github.com/epfml/llm-optimizer-benchmark) harness (or the 2509.02046 protocol).** Justification: both 2025 benchmarking papers show optimizer-family deltas are **largest at 0.1B scale (1.3–1.4x)**, decisively un-saturated; configs, tuned HPs for 24+ optimizers (incl. D-Muon) are public, so you inherit tuned baselines instead of tuning on your budget. A 1% token-efficiency effect ≈ Δloss ~0.002 at loss ~3.3 (local scaling slope); with seed σ ≈ 0.001–0.002 (modded-nanogpt-comparable), n≈4–8/arm resolves it. Cost: 124M @ 2.1B tokens ≈ 3–6 h on one A100 (~$5–9/run spot) — $25 covers ~2 arms x 2 seeds, so use it as the *confirmation* tier after screening on testbed 1, or cut to 0.5x Chinchilla.
3. **CIFAR-10 time-to-target (AlgoPerf-style) instead of accuracy-at-record-steps** — keep airbench's model/data but make the metric **steps (or seconds) to reach 94%** with per-optimizer LR/schedule tuning, i.e., de-couple the co-adapted 200-step budget. Justification: AlgoPerf's design rationale is precisely that time-to-target on a tuned baseline resolves few-percent algorithmic deltas (Shampoo +28%, 2% gap between winners); airbench's 0.14pp std / seconds-scale runs give you hundreds of seeds for ~$5; and the Muon airbench record itself demonstrated headroom on this axis (3.29s → 2.59s, ~21% wall-clock, at fixed 94% target). This is the only sub-$10 option — but expect reviewers to weigh LM evidence far more heavily than CIFAR (per 2509.02046's scale-dependence result).

Avoid: full AlgoPerf (multi-$k), ≥520M LM comparisons (deltas shrink toward 1.1x and cost explodes), and any accuracy-at-fixed-steps metric on a record config (your own evidence plus 2605.06884 says it's ceiling-limited).
