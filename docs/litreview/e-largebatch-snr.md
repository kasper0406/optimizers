# Literature review: per-direction gradient SNR at large batch, and Muon's large-batch mechanism

**Method note:** 14 web searches + 13 source fetches, 2024–2026 prioritized. One fetch (Huang, arXiv:2606.00371) initially returned a summary that mirrored my query phrasing; I re-verified against the abstract and found the paper is actually about Newton–Schulz iteration schedules, *not* direction-selective orthogonalization — that "finding" was fetch-model drift and is excluded. All claims below were cross-checked against abstracts or full-text pages.

## 1) Closest works (citation — contribution — relevance)

1. **Essential AI, "Practical Efficiency of Muon for Pretraining"** ([arXiv:2505.02222](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02222), May 2025) — Muon retains 10–15% token-efficiency advantage over AdamW at batch sizes up to 16M tokens, *beyond* the critical batch size (CBS); expands the compute–time Pareto frontier. **Relevance (b): the anchor empirical result. Crucially, the abstract/paper propose no per-direction or spectral mechanism — the "why" is left open.** A community replication ([marin issue #1565](https://github.com/marin-community/marin/issues/1565)) qualitatively confirms Muon tolerates larger batches than AdamW at 130M/300M scale.

2. **Sato & Iiduka, "Convergence Bound and Critical Batch Size of Muon Optimizer"** ([arXiv:2507.01598](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.01598), Jul 2025) — first theoretical CBS for Muon; batch size mitigates stochastic-noise term in the convergence bound. **Aggregate variance bound only; no per-direction decomposition.** Relevance (b): mechanism at the level of "noise term shrinks with B", nothing finer.

3. **Beneventano, Abdelmoneum, Poggio, "The Spectral Dynamics and Noise Geometry of Muon"** ([arXiv:2606.08388](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08388), Jun 2026) — closest theoretical treatment of Muon-vs-noise. Derives B_crit = 2σ²S(μ)/r where S(μ) = Σ_{i≠j}(σᵢ+σⱼ)⁻² is the polar map's noise sensitivity: small/near-degenerate singular values inflate noise amplification through the Fréchet derivative of the polar factor. Also derives the momentum AR(1) noise factor (1−β)/(1+β). **Explicitly aggregate: noise enters as tr(D_P[Σ]D_Pᵀ), Σ/B isotropic-ish; per-direction SNR is not analyzed** (confirmed in full text). Relevance (a)+(b): the spectral-noise story exists, but only trace-coupled.

4. **Naganuma et al., "Adaptive Batch Sizes Using Non-Euclidean Gradient Noise Scales for Stochastic Sign and Spectral Descent"** ([arXiv:2602.03001](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03001), Feb 2026) — generalizes McCandlish GNS to the dual norms of signSGD (ℓ∞) and Muon-type spectral descent (Schatten-∞); adaptive batch schedule cuts steps up to 66% for Muon/Signum on a 160M Llama. **This is the published state of the art for "gradient noise scale appropriate to Muon" — geometry-aware but still a scalar (per-matrix at best), not per-direction.** Directly relevant to (a) and (b).

5. **Shumaylov et al., "Muon is Not That Special: Random or Inverted Spectra Work Just as Well"** ([arXiv:2605.11181](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.11181), May 2026) — Kaon (random singular values) matches Muon on NanoGPT/GPT-2; optimal spectral exponents fluctuate per batch; **contains the closest published measurement to your instrumentation: batch-gradient agreement (SNR-flavored) resolved along the singular-value spectrum, finding gradients "agree most in the middle of the spectrum, rather than at the extremes."** Frames performance via batch-gradient alignment γₖ vs descent potential Φₖ. Relevance (a): per-spectral-position SNR measured; relevance (b): constrains direction-selective stories — pure geometric/spectral targeting may not be the active ingredient.

6. **Li, Zhang, Liu, Bao, "Denoise First, Orthogonalize Later: Understanding Momentum in Muon via Spectral Filtering"** ([arXiv:2606.03899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03899), Jun 2026) — proves momentum acts as a *spectral filter* under a signal-plus-perturbation gradient model: suppresses perturbation subspace, enlarges signal/noise spectral gap, stabilizes the singular subspaces fed to orthogonalization. **A subspace-level signal-vs-noise mechanism for Muon — but batch-size dependence is not the axis of analysis, and the filtering is implicit (momentum), not measured/routed.** Highly relevant to (b).

7. **Huang et al., "Spectra: Rethinking Optimizers for LLMs Under Spectral Anisotropy"** ([arXiv:2602.11185](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.11185), Jan 2026) — measures a persistent spike (~1.5% of directions) + noise-sensitive tail in gradient spectra at LLaMA-3-8B scale, and **proposes direction-selective adaptation: low-rank spectral shaping that suppresses the spike subspace without amplifying tail noise** (warm-started power iteration); 30% faster convergence than AdamW, beats Muon in their tests. **This is the closest published "direction-selective method at LLM scale." However: selection is by energy/subspace identity, not by temporal statistical classification (no t-statistics, no persistence tests, no per-direction regime labels), and no batch-size connection.**

8. **Ai2, "Revisiting critical batch size for large-batch OLMo pretraining"** ([blog](https://allenai.org/blog/critical-batch-size), 2025) + **"Critical Batch Size Revisited"** ([arXiv:2505.23971](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.23971)) — GNS mispredicts CBS by orders of magnitude for OLMo 1B/7B; blames (i) Adam ≠ SGD scaling and (ii) the well-conditioned (Hessian ≈ identity) assumption. **Relevance (a): published evidence that the *aggregate* noise-scale framework fails precisely because of anisotropy/conditioning — an implicit argument for per-direction analysis that they do not perform.**

9. **DOME: "Improving Signal-to-Noise in SGD via Sharp-Direction Subspace Filtering"** ([arXiv:2507.03545](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.03545), Jul 2025) — identifies a "nuisance subspace" from gradient covariance online, filters it out with little loss impact; motivated by gradient-SNR-sensitive applications. **Per-direction noise identification exists as a measurement+filtering method — but small scale, SGD, no batch-size axis, no signal/oscillation classification.**

10. **Supporting cluster:** "Surge Phenomenon in Optimal LR and Batch Size Scaling" ([arXiv:2405.14578](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14578), NeurIPS 2024 — Adam-style optimal LR is non-monotone in B, aggregate SNR reasoning); noise-covariance-structure works ("On the Overlooked Structure of Stochastic Gradients" [arXiv:2212.02083](https://arxiv.org/html/2212.02083); power-law/bulk-plus-outlier covariance spectra, heavy dimension-wise tails); AdaMuon ([arXiv:2507.11005](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11005)) / NorMuon ([arXiv:2510.05491](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.05491)) / Muon² ([arXiv:2604.09967](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.09967)) — second-moment adaptation *element-wise or neuron-wise after orthogonalization*, i.e., coordinate-basis variance adaptation, **not** singular-direction statistical routing. GONO/PILOT (2025–26) use scalar gradient-direction-consistency signals — parameter- or model-level, not per-singular-direction.

## 2) Verdicts

**(a) Per-direction/per-subspace gradient SNR at large batch — verdict: PARTIALLY reported; the specific premise is OPEN. Confidence: moderately high (broad sweep, but 2026 preprint flood means recall <100%).**
What exists: geometry-aware but scalar noise scales for Muon (Naganuma 2602.03001); SNR resolved along the singular spectrum at GPT-2/NanoGPT scale (Shumaylov 2605.11181); spike-vs-tail signal structure measured at 8B (Spectra 2602.11185); nuisance-subspace covariance estimation (DOME); evidence aggregate GNS fails due to anisotropy (Ai2). What does **not** exist, as far as I found: any published *per-eigendirection critical batch size* or demonstration that per-direction signal statistics (t-tests, persistence classification) transition from unmeasurable to measurable as batch grows — at any scale. Your CIFAR result (per-direction t-stats structurally unmeasurable at B=2000) has no published counterpart or refutation; nobody has published the "noise floor drops at LLM-batch → per-direction signal measurable" claim either way. Best citations: 2605.11181, 2602.03001, Ai2 blog.

**(b) Muon's large-batch advantage tied to per-direction/spectral mechanisms; direction-selective adaptation proposed at LLM scale — verdict: PARTIALLY. Confidence: high on the mechanism gap; moderate on method non-existence.**
The large-batch advantage itself is confirmed and replicated (Essential AI; marin; theory in 2507.01598). Mechanistic accounts exist but are **aggregate-spectral, not per-direction**: polar-map noise-sensitivity CBS (2606.08388), S∞ noise scale (2602.03001), momentum-as-spectral-filter (2606.03899). **No paper found connects the batch-size scaling of Muon's edge to per-direction signal measurability.** Direction-selective *methods* exist at LLM scale (Spectra's spike suppression; implicitly the GaLore/SubTrack subspace family; NorMuon/AdaMuon coordinate-wise variance adaptation), but none does statistical per-direction classification (signal/noise/oscillation regimes) and none is routed by measured per-direction SNR, and none studies the batch-size axis of its own mechanism. Shumaylov et al. is the main *negative* constraint: random spectra matching Muon suggests naive spectral targeting is not the ingredient — any direction-selective proposal must explain why it beats "geometry doesn't matter."

## 3) What constrains the mechanism, and what your instrumentation would add

Published constraints on the Muon-large-batch mechanism: (i) advantage persists *past* CBS where AdamW's linear scaling breaks (Essential AI) — so it is not just "bigger effective batch"; (ii) theory says Muon's noise amplification is governed by polar-map sensitivity S(μ), worst when singular values are small/degenerate — predicting orthogonalization is *more* noise-fragile at small batch and benefits *more* from noise reduction as B grows (2606.08388) — an aggregate prediction consistent with, but never tested by, per-direction measurement; (iii) momentum already performs implicit spectral signal/noise separation (2606.03899); (iv) SNR is spectrally non-uniform, peaking mid-spectrum (2605.11181); (v) aggregate GNS demonstrably mispredicts CBS due to anisotropy (Ai2).

**Genuinely new contributions your per-direction instrumentation would make:** (1) the first *temporal-statistical* classification of individual gradient/momentum directions (persistent-signal t-stats, oscillation detection, regime labels) at LLM scale — every published measurement is energy- or agreement-based snapshots, not longitudinal per-direction hypothesis tests; (2) the first direct test of whether per-direction measurability is a function of batch size — i.e., an empirical *per-direction* critical batch size, which would also adjudicate between the aggregate theories (ii) and (iii) above; (3) if routing then works only in the measurable regime, the first mechanistic bridge from per-direction SNR to Muon's post-CBS advantage. Risk to weigh before spending: the neighborhood is crowded and fast-moving (four directly adjacent papers appeared Jan–Jun 2026), and Shumaylov-style results raise a real possibility that per-direction routing adds nothing beyond what momentum + orthogonalization already implicitly do.
