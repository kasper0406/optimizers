## Literature review: per-layer/per-matrix LR adaptation from temporal gradient statistics

### (1) Closest works

**1. GALA — "Online Learning-guided Learning Rate Adaptation via Gradient Alignment" ([arXiv:2506.08419](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.08419), 2025).** Adapts the LR by tracking alignment between consecutive gradients (plus a local curvature estimate), framed as a 1-D online learning problem (FTRL); LR rises when successive gradients align, falls when they anti-align. **Diff vs ours:** the signal is essentially lag-1 gradient autocorrelation — the same family as our oscillation occupancy — but the granularity is a *single global scalar*, not per-matrix, and it is not connected to Muon/orthogonalized updates. Closest signal match; wrong granularity.

**2. LANTON — "Noise-Adaptive Layerwise Learning Rates" ([arXiv:2510.14009](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14009), 2025).** For LMO/geometry-aware optimizers (explicitly the Muon/Scion family), estimates per-layer gradient *variance in the dual norm* online and assigns time-varying layerwise LRs; LLaMA/GPT experiments. **Diff:** granularity and family match ours (per-layer, online, Muon-adjacent), but the signal is noise *magnitude* (a second-moment/SNR quantity), carrying no serial-correlation or oscillation information. This is the nearest modern neighbor and the one to differentiate against most carefully.

**3. OrScale — "Orthogonalised Optimization with Layer-Wise Trust-Ratio Scaling" ([arXiv:2605.07815](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07815), 2026).** Adds LARS/LAMB-style trust ratios to Muon, with the denominator being the Frobenius norm of the actual orthogonalized update direction; per-step online, CIFAR + FineWeb-Edu 125M–1.1B. **Diff:** exactly the "trust ratio for Muon" slot our proposal targets, but purely *instantaneous norm-based* — no temporal statistics whatsoever. Our proposal is literally the temporal analogue of this paper.

**4. NAMO — "Adam Improves Muon: Adaptive Moment Estimation with Orthogonalized Momentum" ([arXiv:2602.17080](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17080), 2026).** Scales orthogonalized momentum by a *single per-matrix adaptive stepsize* from a norm-based second-moment estimate (NAMO-D adds a neuron-wise diagonal); convergence theory + GPT-2 gains over Muon/AdamW. **Diff:** exact granularity match (one online scalar gain per matrix, in Muon), but the driving signal is Adam-family second moment (norm energy), not autocorrelation/sign-flips/oscillation.

**5. Gluon ([arXiv:2505.13416](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13416), 2025) and successors (e.g., ["Aligning Theory with Practice for Muon-type Optimizers"](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=uic0r409Ys)).** Layer-wise (L⁰,L¹)-smoothness model yielding per-layer stepsizes for Muon/Scion that track fine-tuned values. **Diff:** per-layer and partially gradient-norm-responsive, but curvature/smoothness-derived — the excluded category — and no temporal statistics.

**6. MoLS — "Revealing Modular Gradient Noise Imbalance in LLMs: Calibrating Adam via Signal-to-Noise Ratio" ([arXiv:2605.05794](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05794), 2026).** Online module-level SNR estimates scale Adam updates, replacing hand-tuned module-specific LRs. **Diff:** module granularity ≈ ours; SNR is computed from temporal running moments, so it is the most "temporal" of the norm-family works — but it captures noise magnitude, not serial structure (an AR(−0.8) and an AR(+0.8) stream with equal variance are indistinguishable to SNR but maximally different to our classifier). Adam base, not Muon.

**7. Classics — delta-bar-delta (Jacobs 1988), [Rprop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rprop) (Riedmiller & Braun 1993), vSGD ("No More Pesky Learning Rates", Schaul et al. 2013).** Delta-bar-delta and Rprop adapt step sizes from *consecutive-gradient sign agreement* — precisely the sign-flip signal — and vSGD computes expected-gain LRs from running temporal estimates of gradient mean/variance, with a **per-tensor variant (vSGD-b)**. **Diff:** delta-bar-delta/Rprop are per-parameter, full-batch-era, and unstable under minibatch noise; vSGD-b is per-tensor and temporal but SNR/curvature-based, pre-transformer, and none connect to orthogonalized updates or oscillation-occupancy classification.

**8. Grokfast ([arXiv:2405.20233](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.20233), NeurIPS 2024).** Decomposes gradient trajectories in the *temporal frequency domain* and amplifies slow components via low-pass filtering. **Diff:** genuinely temporal-spectral, but per-parameter, modifies the gradient rather than a per-matrix LR gain, and targets grokking, not Muon-style pretraining. Useful conceptual citation that "temporal frequency content of gradients carries actionable signal."

**9. Q-Ramping — Oscillation-Reduced MXFP4 Training ([arXiv:2502.20853](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2502.20853), 2025).** Periodically *detects and quantifies weight oscillation* and adapts update frequency/effective batch size of oscillating weights. **Diff:** oscillation-detection-driven adaptation exists here, but per-weight, in quantized-training, and it modulates update frequency, not per-matrix LR in full-precision Muon training.

**Other Muon-family granularity census (all non-temporal):** [AdaMuon](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.11005v1) — element-wise second moment on the orthogonalized update + RMS alignment; [NorMuon](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.05491v1) — neuron-wise (row-norm) normalization via low-cost second-order momentum; Moonlight/Muon `adjust_lr` and muP-style rules — *static shape-based* scaling, not measured-signal; [DynMuon](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17109) — spectral shaping UΣᵖVᵀ with p *scheduled* over training, not driven by any online measured statistic; [SGG](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.01049) — per-layer clustering of gradient statistics with cluster-wise scaling (moment-based); MERIT/LAMBC/TVLARS — norm-ratio refinements of LAMB. Adam-mini (blockwise mean second moment, Hessian-structure-motivated), BAdam (block coordinate scheduling, no measured signal), Fira (norm-based scaling) all sit in the excluded norm/second-moment category.

### (2) Novelty verdict

**Unreported as a combination; partially anticipated in components. Confidence: moderately high (~80%).**
- Per-matrix/per-layer *online* LR gain in the Muon family: exists (NAMO, OrScale, LANTON, Gluon) — but always driven by norms, second moments, noise variance, or smoothness, never serial correlation.
- Temporal serial-correlation/sign-flip/oscillation signals driving LR: exist (GALA globally; delta-bar-delta/Rprop per-parameter; Q-Ramping per-weight in QAT) — but never at matrix granularity, never in Muon/orthogonalized optimizers, and never as an occupancy statistic aggregated over a matrix's directions.
- I found **no work that modulates a whole-matrix learning rate by measured temporal autocorrelation or oscillation occupancy of that matrix's gradients/updates**, in any optimizer family, 2024–2026. Caveat: the 2025–26 optimizer literature is extremely high-volume and search-snippet coverage of very recent preprints (esp. OpenReview submissions under review) is imperfect; NAMO and OrScale (both 2026) show this exact granularity slot is being actively populated, so the window may be short.

### (3) Strongest building-upon angle

Frame the method as the **temporal third column of the trust-ratio family**: LARS/LAMB/OrScale scale per-layer LR by *spatial* norm ratios; LANTON/MoLS/NAMO by *noise magnitude*; ours by *serial structure* (oscillation occupancy ≈ implied ηλ overshoot), which the other two signal families provably cannot see (equal-variance AR(+ρ) vs AR(−ρ) argument — a clean, testable separation your WP0.5 synthetic suite already covers). Practically: position against OrScale and NAMO as the direct baselines at identical granularity/cost, adopt Gluon/LANTON's layer-wise LMO framework for a convergence story (their dual-norm machinery extends naturally from variance to autocovariance), and cite delta-bar-delta → GALA as the lineage being lifted from scalar/per-parameter to per-matrix granularity where minibatch noise is averaged over enough directions for the statistic to be well-measured — exactly the "statistically starved per-direction vs well-measured per-matrix" finding from your CIFAR result.
