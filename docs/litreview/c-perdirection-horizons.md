# Literature Report: Per-Direction Spectral Interventions in Matrix Optimizers & the Training-Horizon Caveat

## (1) Closest works (with precise diffs from your approach)

Your approach, for reference: **online per-singular-direction statistical classification (oscillation detection) of Muon's momentum spectrum, with routed gain/damping response**.

**1. COSMOS (Liu et al., arXiv:2502.17410, Feb 2025, Georgia Tech/Microsoft)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2502.17410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.17410)
- *What:* Splits each gradient matrix into the leading eigensubspace vs. remainder; applies SOAP (Adam-in-eigenbasis) to the top subspace and Muon to the bulk. LLM pretraining on transformers; positioned as memory-efficient SOAP-quality training.
- *Diff:* Subspace-selective **treatment**, but the split is by eigenvalue rank (fixed k), not by an online behavioral statistic; response is "which optimizer," not damping/freezing; no oscillation classification. Closest published relative of your architecture, as you suspected.

**2. SOAP / Shampoo family (Vyas et al. 2024; and eigenbasis literature)**
- *What:* The important framing caveat for your novelty claim: SOAP *is* per-eigendirection gain modulation driven by online statistics — it runs Adam's EMA second-moment machinery inside Shampoo's eigenbasis, so every eigendirection gets an individually adapted gain, validated at 360M–660M LLM scale and again in the 2509 benchmarks (best-in-class at high Chinchilla ratios). 
- *Diff:* The statistic is second-moment magnitude (variance normalization), not regime classification (signal/noise/oscillation); the response is continuous rescaling, not routing/freezing; no notion of oscillation detection or ηλ estimation.

**3. DynMuon (arXiv:2605.17109, May 2026)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2605.17109](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17109)
- *What:* Replaces Muon's UV^T with U Σ^p V^T, with p **scheduled globally** (positive early → mildly negative late) based on curvature/noise/training-stage theory. 10.6–26.5% fewer steps than Muon.
- *Verified:* It is **global, not per-direction online**. The exponent p is one scalar per schedule; every direction gets the gain σ_i^p through the same p. No online per-direction statistic, no classification. (Confirms your differentiation.)

**4. Muon^p (Dong et al., arXiv:2606.13867, June 2026)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2606.13867](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13867)
- *What:* Fixed fractional spectral power US^pV^T, p∈(0,1), computed via matmul-only recurrences; Schatten-norm steepest-descent interpretation. Helps mainly for **finetuning** billion-scale models (perplexity + downstream).
- *Diff:* Per-direction gains exist but are a fixed monotone function of σ_i — no statistics, no online adaptation, no damping of specific directions. Same for "Delving into Muon and Beyond" (arXiv:2602.04669), which sweeps p ∈ {0, 1/4, 1/2, 1} uniformly and concludes Muon ≈ spectral normalization, "not universally superior."

**5. SpecMuon — "Muon with Spectral Guidance" (arXiv:2602.16167, Feb 2026)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2602.16167](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16167)
- *What:* The closest thing to per-direction *online* modulation I found: decomposes gradients into singular modes and applies a relaxed scalar auxiliary variable (RSAV) update **individually along dominant spectral directions**, regulating per-mode step sizes via a loss-energy statistic, with energy-dissipation/stability theory.
- *Diff & scale:* Domain is **SciML only** (PINNs, DeepONets, Burgers/fractional PDEs) — tiny networks, short horizons; the statistic is global loss energy fed through per-mode auxiliary variables, not per-direction time-series classification; motivation is stiffness control, not oscillation routing. Nothing at LLM or even CIFAR-benchmark scale.

**6. Dion (Ahn, Xu et al., arXiv:2504.05295, Microsoft, 2025)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2504.05295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.05295)
- *What:* Orthonormalizes only the top-r singular directions of momentum (amortized power iteration); the *discarded* (bulk) directions are handled by **error feedback folded back into momentum**, so they're deferred, not dropped. Trains LLMs at scale with rank fractions well below 1 with minimal quality loss.
- *Diff:* Rank-based hard truncation + error feedback; no statistic beyond singular-value rank; no damping/oscillation logic. Empirically relevant to you: it shows bulk directions can be *delayed* at LLM scale without much harm — evidence that per-direction differentiation is at least survivable at scale. See also Orth-Dion (arXiv:2605.16341).

**7. Aurora (Tilde Research, arXiv:2606.27715, June 2026)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2606.27715](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27715)
- *What:* Leverage-aware modification of Muon enforcing row-uniformity of updates while staying near the polar factor; motivated by a self-reinforcing "starved neuron" feedback loop. SOTA on the modded-nanogpt speedrun optimizer track.
- *Diff:* Per-**row** (neuron), not per-singular-direction; statistic is row-norm/leverage, not temporal behavior. Similarly **NorMuon** (arXiv:2510.05491, per-neuron second-moment normalization after orthogonalization, ~15% fewer steps at LLM scale) and **AdaMuon** (arXiv:2507.11005, element-wise second moments on the orthogonalized update) — these show *post-orthogonalization statistical reweighting* helps at LLM scale, but all in coordinate/neuron bases, never the singular basis.

**8. "Does SGD really happen in tiny subspaces?" (Song, Ahn, Yun, arXiv:2405.16002, ICLR 2025)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2405.16002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.16002)
- *What:* The key **interventional** subspace result in the EOS-adjacent literature: projecting updates *onto* the dominant Hessian subspace stalls training; projecting the dominant subspace *out* (keeping only bulk) trains **as well as the full update**, across MLP/CNN/Transformer, MNIST/CIFAR/SST2, including EOS regime, momentum, and adaptive optimizers. Gradient–dominant-subspace alignment is shown to be spurious (SGD noise).
- *Relevance:* Small-scale, short-ish horizons — but it is direct published evidence that **removing/damping top-curvature directions is at best neutral for loss** at short horizons, i.e., broadly consistent with your null. Related non-interventional theory: Damian et al. "Self-Stabilization" (2022) — GD at EOS *already* implements negative feedback on the top eigendirection by itself, which is a principled reason externally damping oscillating directions may be redundant. Also "Edge Flow" (arXiv:2606.18080) models the oscillation direction/magnitude explicitly but doesn't intervene.

**9. Cautious-optimizer family (Liang et al., arXiv:2411.16085; masking follow-ups arXiv:2602.15322)** 
- *What:* Per-**coordinate** gating by an online sign-agreement statistic (mask update where momentum and gradient disagree — i.e., crude per-coordinate oscillation gating). One line of code; consistent small gains in LLM pretraining.
- *Diff:* Coordinate basis, binary statistic; but it is the main published evidence that *statistic-gated per-direction masking of updates can help at LLM scale* — just never in the spectral basis.

**10. "Spectral Dynamics and Noise Geometry of Muon" (arXiv:2606.08388) and "Stability of Singular Distribution" (arXiv:2605.26489)** — measurement papers, no interventions, but both directly relevant background: the former shows Muon's flat-spectrum bias is **regime-dependent** (helped nanoGPT, hurt a small ViT); the latter shows the normalized singular spectrum stabilizes early and that spectral constraints bind **only in the long slow-descent phase** — see (b).

## (2) Verdict for (a)

**Per-direction ONLINE statistical routing/classification in spectral optimizers is, to the best of this search, NOT yet published.** Nothing found — across Muon variants, Shampoo/SOAP lineage, Dion/GaLore lineage, or the 2025–2026 spectral-shaping wave — classifies individual singular directions by temporal statistics (oscillation/signal/noise regimes) and routes different responses online. The occupied neighboring cells are: rank-based subspace splits with different optimizers per subspace (COSMOS), rank truncation + error feedback (Dion), globally-scheduled spectral exponents (DynMuon, Muon^p, 2602.04669), variance-statistic per-eigendirection scaling (SOAP/Shampoo — the closest in spirit, at LLM scale), per-neuron/per-coordinate statistical reweighting or gating (NorMuon, AdaMuon, Aurora, Cautious), and per-mode auxiliary-variable step control in SciML only (SpecMuon). Your specific combination (temporal per-direction statistic → classification → routed damping, in the momentum singular basis) appears open. Caveat: SpecMuon and Aurora show the field is actively converging on this territory in 2026; and no one has published per-direction *oscillation damping* results at LLM scale — your airbench null is also not contradicted by anything published.

## (3) Verdict for (b) — the horizon caveat

**Moderately strong published support, including optimizer-specific instances; no exact "null-at-short-horizon → positive-at-long-horizon" case for a spectral intervention, but several adjacent ones.** Best citations, strongest first:

- **"Fantastic Pretraining Optimizers and Where to Find Them" (Wen et al., arXiv:2509.02046)** — the canonical optimizer-specific statement: *"loss curves of different optimizers may cross multiple times... judging optimizers using intermediate checkpoints may result in a different ranking than comparing models at the target training budget"*; rankings also flip with Chinchilla ratio (Muon best at 1×, overtaken by SOAP/Kron at 8×). Direct published support that short-horizon optimizer evaluation is unreliable **in both directions** — note this cuts against over-reading your null but also warns that a long-horizon positive is speculative, not implied. Companion: "Benchmarking Optimizers for LLM Pretraining" (arXiv:2509.01440).
- **"Stability of Singular Distribution" (arXiv:2605.26489)** — the best mechanistic support for your specific caveat: the normalized singular spectrum stabilizes early, and loss-decrease becomes bounded by singular-distribution variation **only in the prolonged slow phase**; Muon/WSD are interpreted through their modulation of this quantity. A 200-step airbench run plausibly never leaves phase 1, where this analysis says spectral structure is not yet the binding constraint. This is an argument, not a demonstrated crossover.
- **Wu et al., "Understanding Short-Horizon Bias in Stochastic Meta-Optimization" (ICLR 2018, arXiv:1803.02021)** — the classic compounding argument: greedy short-horizon objectives systematically favor over-damped/small-step behavior that loses at long horizons (noisy-quadratic analysis). Directly applicable to damping interventions: extra damping is exactly the kind of change short horizons over-reward — meaning a *null* (not even a win) for damping at 200 steps is, if anything, mildly discouraging under this lens.
- **Kaddour et al., "No Train No Gain" (NeurIPS 2023, arXiv:2307.06440)** — the mirror-image caution: efficient-training/optimizer gains measured at short compute-matched budgets **vanish** with fully-decayed baselines at longer ones; horizon effects more often delete gains than create them.
- **"Hyperparameter Transfer Enables Consistent Gains of Matrix-Preconditioned Optimizers Across Scales" (arXiv:2512.05620)** — counterpoint showing scale/horizon-dependent conclusions about matrix optimizers are themselves fragile to tuning methodology.
- Muon^p (above) helping at **finetuning** but not clearly at pretraining, and Muon helping nanoGPT but hurting ViT (arXiv:2606.08388), are further published instances of spectral-treatment benefits being regime-dependent rather than universal.

**Net:** the "wrong horizon" caveat is defensible with citations (rankings provably flip with horizon; spectral constraints argued to bind only late), but there is no published example of a per-direction spectral intervention that was null short and positive long — and Song et al. 2405.16002 plus Damian et al.'s self-stabilization give principled reasons the top-direction oscillation may be self-correcting or loss-irrelevant at any horizon.
