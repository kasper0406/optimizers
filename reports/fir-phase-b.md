# Program #9 Phase B results — the anti-correlation is optimizer-endogenous

2026-07-22. Pre-registration: `reports/fir-phase-a.md` §2 (commit a5b026a,
before any run). 140 runs, seeds 1442–1451 (n = 10 paired), airbench_smoke
record recipe, single healthy GPU (the box's flaky card hard-faulted
mid-sweep; resumed via done-check, zero retries after). Endpoint
tta_val_acc.

## 1. Results vs predictions

Best-vs-best (equal tuning effort: stock swept β ∈ {0.45, 0.6, 0.75},
FIR swept τ ∈ {0.8, 1.5, 2.5}):

| lr | stock best | FIR-white best | Δ paired | FIR-measured | Δ paired |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.24 (1×) | 0.9393 (β=0.6) | 0.9342 (τ=1.5) | **−0.510pp ± 0.068** | 0.9339 | −0.537pp ± 0.061 |
| 0.48 (2×) | 0.9359 (β=0.45) | 0.9179 (τ=0.8) | **−1.804pp ± 0.076** | 0.9170 | −1.898pp ± 0.091 |

- **P1: outcome (c)** — the min-variance kernel loses decisively at 1×;
  the pre-registered reading applies: the extrapolation property of the
  Nesterov kernel (which the variance-at-matched-lag proxy cannot see)
  dominates the 2.5–3.4× variance advantage.
- **P2: refuted** — FIR degrades *more* at 2×, not less. Notable side
  observation: stock's own β re-tune is a real hot-LR rescue (β 0.6→0.45
  at 2× is +0.17pp), consistent with the inexactness–stepsize–momentum
  coupling literature.
- **P3: confirmed** — measured-ρ ≈ white within 0.03–0.09pp at matched τ,
  exactly as the offline decomposition predicted.
- **P4: the finding.** The ρ̂₁ measured *inside* FIRMuon is **positive**
  (median +0.10, range +0.06…+0.22 across matrices) — while the same
  statistic under stock Muon dynamics is −0.3…−0.5 (program #8) and the
  per-direction ACF under stock is −0.29 (kill-test). **The negative
  serial correlation is endogenous to the optimizer's closed loop** — it
  is generated by the EMA/Nesterov overshoot dynamics, not a property of
  the gradient noise. Changing the kernel changes the process; the
  statistic the filter was designed to exploit ceases to exist under the
  filter. The offline kill-test's stationarity assumption (process fixed
  under filter change) is precisely what failed.
- **P5: moot** given P1(c) magnitude (the deficit is not an early-phase
  transient artifact; all τ cells lose similarly).

## 2. What this closes and what it opens

Closed: variance-optimal linear filtering of the momentum stream against
the *measured open-loop* autocovariance — in either fixed (white-optimal)
or adaptive (measured-ρ) form — is not an improvement direction on this
substrate. The 2.3–4.7× estimator-variance headroom is real as
measurement and worthless as intervention, because the measured process
is a closed-loop response characteristic, not exogenous noise. Any future
"optimizer as filter" design must model the loop (gradient depends on the
filter through the iterates), not the marginal statistics. This also
gives the mechanism behind two prior observations: why the EMA family
survives (its "suboptimal" kernel is part of a critically-damped loop),
and why program #8's healthy-regime signature is *deep* negative ρ̂
(coherent oscillation = a well-driven system at the edge, echoing the
WP1.2 population and the §3.2 mechanism probes).

Opened (analysis-first, no compute claim): the closed-loop view suggests
the right object is the loop transfer function, not the filter — i.e.,
per-direction dynamics of (filter, landscape) jointly. That is a theory
question adjacent to the non-Euclidean EoS line, and it is what a
principled successor to both programs #8 and #9 would need.
