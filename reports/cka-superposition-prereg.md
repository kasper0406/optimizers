## The candidate law (program #13 Stage-2, exploratory then; test now)

Same-seed endpoint representation drift (linear CKA to the batch's
peak-rung run) superposes across batches as a function of *relative* LR
position: at B ∈ {1000, 2000, 4000} the drift-vs-rung-index curves
coincide within ~0.003 CKA at every one of six ×1.15-spaced rungs
(0.94 → 0.91). If lawful, drift is a batch-free gauge of effective LR
dose — a tuning-transfer instrument.

## Held-out test

B = 500 (outside the fitted range), relative ladder lr = lr_peak(500) ×
1.15^k, k = 0…6, with lr_peak(500) = 0.24 × (500/1000)^0.35 ≈ 0.187
(the fitted frontier law supplies the anchor — this also makes the test
jointly a check of the law's anchor). Seeds 1496–1505, endpoint hooks:
70 runs ≈ 12 GPU-min.

## Pre-registered prediction and bars

- **P1 (superposition):** the B = 500 drift-vs-k curve lies within the
  envelope of the B ∈ {1000, 2000, 4000} curves widened by 2× the
  cross-batch spread observed among those three (per-rung bar:
  |CKA_500(k) − mean_others(k)| ≤ 2 × sd_others(k), all k ≥ 1; with
  sd_others floored at the replicate CKA noise). Pass = the law
  survives extrapolation to a 2× smaller batch; refuted if ≥ 2 rungs
  breach 3× the spread.
- **P2 (anchor check, descriptive):** the accuracy-peak rung at B = 500
  falls within one rung of k = 0 (the law-derived anchor); a shifted
  peak displaces the whole curve and is reported as an anchor error,
  separately from P1's shape question.
